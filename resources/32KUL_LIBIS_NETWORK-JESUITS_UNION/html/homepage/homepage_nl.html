Jesuit Armarium Book Collections of the Jesuits of the European Low Countries

The Society of Jesus in the Low Countries owns today an impressive collection of ca. half a million books, which is managed by, among others, the following institutions:

  • KU Leuven Libraries – Maurits Sabbe Library
  • KADOC – KU Leuven
  • Library of the Ruusbroec Institute

The collection was formed by and is property of the Jesuit Region of the European Low Countries, whether the subcollections were formerly held at historically important libraries such as the theological library at Heverlee (Flemish Jesuits) or the Berchmanianum at Nijmegen (Dutch Jesuits), or systematically collected by Jesuit organizations in the wake of their research (e.g. Ruusbroec Institute).

The present discovery platform Jesuit Armarium aims to virtually unite all these books (including the precious books, manuscripts, journals, etc. ) of this Jesuit region and to lead researchers and enthusiasts to their current place of safekeeping.

For more information, please contact jesuitarmarium@kuleuven.be

Be advised, this platform is a work in progress and does not claim to hold an exhaustive account of all Jesuit library property of the ELC region.
